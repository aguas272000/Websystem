Culture

Belgium is known for its rich cultural heritage, influenced by its diverse history and unique position at the crossroads of Europe. The country is renowned for its art, architecture, and cuisine, with a strong tradition of beer brewing, chocolate making, and comic art. Belgians take pride in their festivals, such as the vibrant Carnaval de Binche and the colorful Flower Carpet in Brussels, showcasing their love for celebration and community. Additionally, Belgium is home to multiple UNESCO World Heritage sites, reflecting its commitment to preserving its cultural landmarks and traditions for future generations.

Beer Culture - Belgian beer culture is deeply rooted in tradition and craftsmanship, with a wide variety of beer styles and brewing techniques. From Trappist ales brewed by monks to sour lambics aged in oak barrels, Belgian beers are celebrated for their complexity, flavor profiles, and strong ties to local communities and history.

Binche is a historic town in Belgium known for its iconic Carnaval de Binche, a UNESCO-recognized event featuring colorful parades, traditional costumes, and lively music. The town is also famous for its ornate Grand Place, charming cobblestone streets, and the impressive Binche Belfry, offering visitors a glimpse into Belgium's rich cultural heritage.

Carnival of Aalst The Carnival of Aalst in Belgium is a vibrant and colorful festival known for its satirical and irreverent parade floats, costumes, and performances. It has a long history dating back to the Middle Ages and is recognized as a UNESCO Intangible Cultural Heritage of Humanity, attracting visitors from around the world to experience its unique blend of humor, tradition, and creativity.

Falconry in Belgium is a traditional hunting sport that dates back centuries, involving the use of trained birds of prey to hunt small game. It is considered a cultural heritage activity in Belgium, with dedicated falconers practicing the art of training and flying birds of prey in a sustainable and ethical manner.

Giants and DragonsThe Procession of Giants and Dragons is a traditional event in Belgium where large puppets representing historical or mythical figures are paraded through the streets. These processions often feature elaborate costumes, music, and performances, showcasing the country's folklore and cultural heritage in a festive and colorful manner.

Shrimp fishing on horseback in Belgium is a unique tradition that dates back to the 16th century, primarily practiced in the coastal town of Oostduinkerke. Fishermen ride horses into the shallow waters of the North Sea, using special nets to catch shrimp, showcasing a sustainable and traditional method of harvesting seafood.
