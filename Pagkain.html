Food

Belgium, a country in Western Europe, is known for medieval towns, Renaissance architecture and as headquarters of the European Union and NATO. The country has distinctive regions including Dutch-speaking Flanders to the north, French-speaking Wallonia to the south and a German-speaking community to the east

Moules-frites is a classic Belgian dish consisting of mussels cooked in a broth of white wine, garlic, and herbs, served with a side of crispy fries. It is a popular and iconic dish in Belgium, enjoyed for its simplicity and delicious flavors.

Carbonade Flamande is a traditional Belgian beef stew made with beer, onions, and spices. It is slow-cooked until the beef is tender and flavorful, often served with fries or bread for soaking up the rich sauce.

Stoemp is a traditional Belgian dish consisting of mashed potatoes mixed with vegetables such as carrots, Brussels sprouts, or leeks. It is often served alongside sausages or meatballs, making it a hearty and comforting meal.

Gentse Waterzooi is a traditional Belgian dish originating from the city of Ghent. It is a creamy stew made with fish or chicken, vegetables, and herbs, typically served with bread or potatoes.

Paling in' t Groen is a traditional Belgian dish, consisting of eel cooked in a savory green herb sauce. It is a popular dish in the Flanders region and is often served with boiled potatoes.

Brussels waffles, also known as Belgian waffles, are a type of waffle that is light, crispy, and fluffy on the inside. They are typically larger and have deeper pockets compared to traditional American waffles, making them perfect for holding toppings like whipped cream, fruits, or chocolate sauce.
