Destinations

Belgium, a country in Western Europe, is known for medieval towns, Renaissance architecture and as headquarters of the European Union and NATO. The country has distinctive regions including Dutch-speaking Flanders to the north, French-speaking Wallonia to the south and a German-speaking community to the east

The Antwerp Cathedral, also known as the Cathedral of Our Lady, is a stunning Gothic church located in Antwerp, Belgium. It is one of the largest and most important cathedrals in the country, known for its impressive architecture and collection of artwork, including paintings by Peter Paul Rubens.

Atomium is a landmark building in Brussels, Belgium originally constructed for the 1958 World's Fair. It is designed to resemble a unit cell of an iron crystal, magnified 165 billion times.

Bruges is a stunning medieval city in Belgium known for its beautifully preserved medieval houses, characterized by their stepped gables and intricate facades. These buildings transport visitors back in time, offering a glimpse into the architectural history of the region.

The Grand Place in Brussels, Belgium, is one of the most famous squares in Europe and a UNESCO World Heritage Site. It is surrounded by stunning architecture including the Town Hall, the King's House, and guildhalls. The square is a hub of activity with markets, festivals, and events often taking place there. It is a must-see destination for visitors to Brussels.

Ghent's St. Michael's Bridge is a historic stone bridge spanning the River Leie, offering stunning views of the city's medieval architecture and iconic buildings. The bridge is a popular spot for tourists and locals alike to take photographs and soak in the picturesque scenery of Ghent.

Leuven Town Hall, located in the Flemish Brabant province of Belgium, is a stunning example of Brabantine Gothic architecture. Built in the 15th century, it features intricate stone carvings, statues, and a soaring bell tower, making it a must-see landmark in the city.
